# Constitutional Provisions on Anti-Corruption

## Constitution of Nepal, 2072 (2015)

### Part 21: Commission for the Investigation of Abuse of Authority

- **Article 238**: Establishment of CIAA as a constitutional body
- **Article 239**: Functions, duties, and powers of CIAA
  - Investigate abuse of authority by persons holding public office
  - Investigate improper conduct or corruption
  - Recommend departmental action or file cases in court
- **Article 240**: Appointment of Chief Commissioner and Commissioners
- **Article 241**: Terms of office and conditions of service
- **Article 242**: Procedures and annual reporting

### Part 11: Judiciary
- **Article 127**: Supreme Court — final appellate authority for Special Court decisions
- **Article 139**: High Courts — may hear certain corruption-related writs

### Fundamental Rights Relevant to Anti-Corruption

- **Article 27**: Right to Information — citizens can access government information, supporting transparency
- **Article 30**: Right to Clean Environment — relates to public resource protection
- **Article 46**: Right to Social Justice — includes protection against exploitation

## Constitutional Principles

### Good Governance
- The State is directed to maintain good governance by preventing corruption
- Public offices must be transparent and accountable

### Directive Principles
- The State shall pursue policies to eliminate corruption and irregularities
- Public procurement must be transparent and competitive

### Accountability
- All public officials are accountable for their actions
- Independent constitutional bodies oversee government accountability

## Relationship with International Law

Nepal is a signatory to:
- United Nations Convention against Corruption (UNCAC), ratified 2011
- These international commitments inform domestic anti-corruption law
