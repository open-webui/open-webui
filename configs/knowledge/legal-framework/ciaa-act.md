# Commission for the Investigation of Abuse of Authority (CIAA)

## Constitutional Basis

The CIAA is established under Part 21 of the Constitution of Nepal (2072) as a constitutional body responsible for investigating corruption and abuse of authority.

## Legal Framework

### CIAA Act, 2048 (1991)
- Establishes the Commission's structure, powers, and procedures
- Defines the scope of investigation authority
- Provides for the appointment of Commissioners (Chief Commissioner + Commissioners)

## Powers and Functions

### Investigation
- Investigate complaints of corruption and abuse of authority
- Can initiate investigations suo moto (on its own)
- Power to summon witnesses, require documents, and conduct searches

### Prosecution
- File cases at the Special Court, Kathmandu
- Represent the prosecution in corruption cases
- Can appeal Special Court decisions to the Supreme Court

### Preventive Measures
- Recommend improvements to prevent corruption
- Raise public awareness about corruption
- Monitor public procurement and government transactions

## Jurisdiction
- All public officials in Nepal
- Government-owned corporations and entities
- Persons receiving government funds or concessions

## Filing a Complaint
- Written complaint with supporting evidence
- Can be filed by any person with knowledge of corruption
- CIAA also accepts anonymous complaints
- Complaints can be filed at CIAA central office or district offices

## CIAA Case Number Format
CIAA cases use the format: `YYY-CR-NNNN` where:
- YYY = Nepali year (e.g., 081 for 2081 BS)
- CR = Case type code
- NNNN = Sequential case number
