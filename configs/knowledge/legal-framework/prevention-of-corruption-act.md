# Prevention of Corruption Act, 2059 (2002)

## Overview

The Prevention of Corruption Act, 2059 (2002) is Nepal's primary anti-corruption legislation. It criminalizes bribery, abuse of authority, illicit enrichment, and related offenses by public officials.

## Key Provisions

### Definition of Public Official
"Public official" includes government employees, constitutional body members, employees of government-owned corporations, and any person performing public duties.

### Offenses
- **Bribery** (Section 3): Giving or taking bribes in connection with official duties
- **Abuse of Authority** (Section 4): Causing loss to the government or benefit to oneself through abuse of position
- **Illicit Enrichment** (Section 5): Possessing assets disproportionate to known sources of income
- **False Claims** (Section 6): Submitting false claims for payment or benefits
- **Revenue Leakage** (Section 7): Causing loss of government revenue

### Penalties
- Fine equal to the amount of graft or loss, plus imprisonment
- Confiscation of property acquired through corruption
- Disqualification from public office

### Special Court
Cases under this Act are adjudicated by the Special Court, Kathmandu, with appeal to the Supreme Court.

### Statute of Limitations
- Complaint must be filed within 5 years of knowledge of the offense (Section 22)

## Investigation Authority

The Commission for the Investigation of Abuse of Authority (CIAA) is the primary investigating agency for corruption cases in Nepal, established under the Constitution of Nepal.
