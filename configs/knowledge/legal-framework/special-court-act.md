# Special Court, Nepal

## Establishment

The Special Court is established under the Special Court Act, 2059 (2002) to adjudicate corruption cases filed by the CIAA.

## Jurisdiction

- All cases filed under the Prevention of Corruption Act, 2059
- Cases under other specific laws referred to the Special Court
- Jurisdiction extends throughout Nepal

## Structure

### Bench Composition
- Presided over by a Chairperson and two Members
- Chairperson and Members are appointed by the Government of Nepal on recommendation of the Judicial Council
- Must have qualification of a High Court judge

### Seat
- The Special Court is located in Kathmandu
- It may hold hearings at other locations as needed

## Procedure

### Case Filing
- CIAA files a charge sheet at the Special Court
- The charge sheet must specify the offense, evidence, and the accused persons

### Trial Process
1. Charge sheet filing
2. Accused appears and responds to charges
3. Evidence examination (prosecution and defense)
4. Final arguments
5. Judgment (verdict)

### Time Frame
- The Special Court should complete proceedings within the time specified by law
- Extension may be granted for complex cases

### Appeal
- Decisions of the Special Court can be appealed to the Supreme Court
- Appeal must be filed within 35 days of the judgment

## Key Case Types
- Cases challenging public procurement decisions
- Illicit enrichment cases
- Bribery and abuse of authority cases
- Revenue-related corruption cases
- Cases involving public land or property

## Verdict
- The verdict can be conviction (with sentence), acquittal, or partial acquittal
- Convictions specify imprisonment term, fine amount, and confiscation orders
- Verdicts are public documents
